Climate Change And The Global Economy

What is Climate Change?

According to the United Nations, Climate Change is “long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. These shifts may be natural, such as through variations in the solar cycle. But since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to burning fossil fuels like coal, oil and gas.”

Why are we interested in investigating Climate Change?

Climate changes could cause ice melting in the Artic, low-lying communities being submerged underwater, hotter overall climate, food shortages, and more extreme weather patterns. These changes are all very avoidable as human behavior is the main driver of climate change. As a result, we wanted to investigate which communities are most vulnerable to the impacts of climate change and which communities are contributing the most to the causes.

How will we measure Climate Change Risk?

We are measuring two things: who is most affected, and who is causing the most damage.To measure who is being affected we will investigate data that analyzes. To measure who is causing the most damage we must understand what factors contribute the most to climate change. As stated by the UN, climate change has been most drastically affected by the “burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil and gas.” As a result, we will look at a breakdown in which companies and countries are burning the most fossil fuels.

Vulnerability To Climate Change

We investigated the economic risks of climate-related extreme weather events that countries faced between 1998 and 2017. Results showed that the United States is at high risk despite being one of the wealthiest countries, while less wealthy South Sudan and Tanzania are relatively safe.
